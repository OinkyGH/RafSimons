RAF SIMONS

FW2001 – Riot! Riot! Riot!

Raf Simons’ Fall/Winter 2001 collection, titled “Riot! Riot! Riot!”, stands as a seminal moment in fashion history. Marking his return after a year-long hiatus, Simons explored youth rebellion, counterculture, and dystopian aesthetics. Presented in a dimly lit warehouse with industrial scaffolding and fog, the show’s oversized garments and obscured faces embodied a powerful sense of anonymity and defiance.

Central to this collection was the iconic MA-1 camouflage bomber jacket. Originally a standard military piece by Fostex, Simons transformed it with patches of cultural significance—images of David Bowie, Manic Street Preachers’ Richey Edwards, and Bauhaus posters—infusing each jacket with a narrative of resistance and artistic expression.

Its rarity and cultural resonance have elevated the “Riot” bomber to coveted status among collectors. While retailing at around €400 originally, it now commands up to $52,000 on resale platforms like Grailed, driven by its scarcity, historic importance, and high-profile wearers such as Kanye West and Drake.

“Design is—what I do best. It’s a way of thinking, not just dressing.”
— Raf Simons, 2002

By merging punk-inspired graphics with minimalist tailoring and socio-political commentary, the “Riot! Riot! Riot!” collection redefined menswear silhouettes and demonstrated fashion’s power as a medium for storytelling. Today, it remains a landmark example of how clothing can channel cultural ideas and resonate across generations.
