AW2004 – Waves

Raf Simons’ Autumn/Winter 2004 collection, titled “Waves,” marked a departure from overt youth subcultures toward a meditation on form, volume, and the tension between freedom and restraint.

The runway was stripped back—no elaborate sets or props—so that sharp lighting highlighted garments sculpted with sweeping lines, oversized shoulders, and layered drapery.

Signature pieces included a bonded neoprene wetsuit jacket with zippered panels that echoed surf gear, and a series of technical cargo coats featuring exaggerated pockets and harness-like straps.

Knitwear played a pivotal role too: chunky rollnecks and patchwork crewnecks blended soft alpaca with rigid technical yarns, suggesting both comfort and controlled containment.

“Waves is about being held and pushed—embracing tension as its own form of release.”
— Raf Simons, AW2004 show notes

The color palette was muted—slate grays, deep navies, and washed indigos—accented by occasional flashes of phosphorescent yellow along seams and zippers.

Footwear and accessories followed suit: oversized leather boots with industrial buckles and sculptural backpacks built from coated canvas reinforced the collection’s ethos of purposeful enclosure.

“Waves” has since been celebrated for its rigorous balance of architecture and wearability, laying groundwork for contemporary explorations of utility and form in menswear.
