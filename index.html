Biography

[PORTRAIT OF RAF SIMONS]

Raf Simons, 2020

The Architect of Youth Culture

1968

Born in Neerpelt, Belgium. Trained as industrial designer at LUCA School of Arts, Genk.

1991

Graduated in Industrial & Furniture Design in Genk, Belgium.

1995

Launched eponymous menswear label after internship with Walter Van Beirendonck. Debut collection featured slim silhouettes that would redefine menswear.

2000-2005

"Golden Era" producing iconic collections like Riot Riot (FW01) and Closer (FW03), merging punk aesthetics with minimalist tailoring.

2005-2012

Creative Director at Jil Sander, introducing vibrant colors to the minimalist house while maintaining his rebellious edge.

2012-2015

Became co-creative director at Dior, bringing his subversive approach to haute couture.

2016-2018

Chief Creative Officer at Calvin Klein 205W39NYC, then refocusing on his own label while collaborating with brands like Eastpak, Dr. Martens, and Adidas.

2020-Present

Became Co-Creative Director of Prada, working alongside Miuccia Prada. (Read more)

November 2023

Raf Simons officially shuts down his namesake brand, after 27 years of operation. (Official announcement)

"I'm not interested in fashion. I'm interested in ideas that can be connected to fashion."

— Raf Simons, 2003

Design Philosophy

Simons' work synthesizes:

  • Youth subcultures (punk, rave, mod)
  • Industrial design principles
  • Artistic collaborations (Sterling Ruby, Peter Saville)
  • Deconstruction of traditional tailoring
