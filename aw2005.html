AW2005 – History of My World

Raf Simons’ Autumn/Winter 2005 collection, titled “History of My World,” built on the innovations of his previous seasons to craft a forward-looking narrative of personal and cultural evolution.

Presented on a minimalist, prop-free runway, the show highlighted radical new shapes: voluminous trousers paired with cropped jackets featuring gathered waistlines and exaggerated paper-bag silhouettes, often cinched by wide leather corset belts.

Military and uniform references surfaced throughout, with acid-washed denim jackets and field coats cut with broad shoulders tapering at the waist—mid-century tunic shapes reimagined through avant-garde tailoring.

Central to the season was the “Poltergeist” parka, patched with horror-film imagery, haunted-house graphics, and cryptic text in collaboration with Peter de Potter, fusing cinematic terror with sartorial craftsmanship.

Knitwear employed distressed patchwork techniques on wool–elastane blends, resulting in turtlenecks and crewnecks that appeared individually time-worn and richly textured.

Accessories and footwear reinforced the collection’s themes: high-top sneakers with reflective straps, sculptural backpacks in coated canvas, and wide leather belts with industrial buckles underscored the tension between utility and refinement.

“This season is about mapping the past onto our present selves—embracing what we inherit while inventing our own histories.”
— Raf Simons, AW2005 show notes

The color palette remained austere—gunmetal grays, washed indigos, and deep blacks—interrupted only by occasional flashes of phosphorescent yellow along seams and zippers to suggest a spectral glow.

Though initially met with mixed reactions, “History of My World” has become celebrated for its conceptual rigor and emotional depth, with archival pieces commanding significant prices on the secondary market.
