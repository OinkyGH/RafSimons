Biography

Raf Simons, 2018

The Architect of Youth Culture

1968

Born in Neerpelt, Belgium. Trained as industrial designer at LUCA School of Arts, Genk.

1991

Graduated in Industrial & Furniture Design in Genk, Belgium.

1995

Launched eponymous menswear label after internship with Walter Van Beirendonck. Debut collection featured slim silhouettes that would redefine menswear.

2000-2005

"Golden Era" producing iconic collections like Riot Riot (FW01) and Closer (FW03), merging punk aesthetics with minimalist tailoring.

2005-2012

Creative Director at Jil Sander, introducing vibrant colors to the minimalist house while maintaining his rebellious edge.

2012-2015

Became co-creative director at Dior, bringing his subversive approach to haute couture.

2016-2018

Chief Creative Officer at Calvin Klein 205W39NYC, then refocusing on his own label while collaborating with brands like Eastpak, Dr. Martens, and Adidas.

2020-Present

Became Co-Creative Director of Prada, working alongside Miuccia Prada. (Read more)

November 2023

Raf Simons officially shuts down his namesake brand, after 27 years of operation. (Official announcement)

Why Raf Simons?

Raf Simons revolutionized menswear by channeling youth subcultures—punk, rave and post-punk—into sharply tailored silhouettes, creating an aesthetic both intellectual and emotionally charged. His early collections were met with skepticism by the fashion establishment, who found his street-inspired graphics and minimalist cuts too radical, but young buyers quickly recognized an authenticity missing elsewhere.

Over time, as his “Riot Riot Riot” bomber and Joy Division-referencing pieces became icons of avant-garde design, demand exploded. Archive scarcity and cult status drove secondary-market prices into the tens of thousands, underpinned by a classic imbalance of supply and demand. Today, original Raf Simons garments fetch record sums at auction and resale platforms, reflecting both their historical importance and enduring influence on modern streetwear.

Critics now hail Simons as an “instigator” who forced luxury brands to rethink their formulas, culminating in high-profile roles at Jil Sander, Dior, Calvin Klein and Prada. What was once dismissed as youth rebellion is now taught in design schools as a masterclass in cultural narrative—making Raf Simons not just a designer, but a defining voice of contemporary fashion.
