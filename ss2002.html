RAF SIMONS

SS2002 – Woe Unto Those Who Spit on the Fear Generation...

Raf Simons’ Spring/Summer 2002 collection, known by its evocative title, explored the aftermath of political unrest and societal fear in a post-9/11 world. The collection combined dystopian utilitarian aesthetics with layered construction and graphic prints expressing anxiety and unease.

Drawing from industrial workwear and military influences, Simons created garments with strong silhouettes but incorporated graphics and slogans that referenced political and cultural tensions of the early 2000s. The use of muted colors, often shades of gray and olive, emphasized the somber mood.

“Clothing can carry the weight of the times – it’s a language of resistance.”
— Raf Simons, SS2002 Interview

The collection’s nuanced commentary on fear and control has made it a touchstone in understanding fashion's role in reflecting and challenging social climates. Today, it is celebrated as a profound example of Simons’ visionary design approach.
